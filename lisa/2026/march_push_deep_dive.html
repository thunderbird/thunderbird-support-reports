APush delivery failure — the core bug

These 34 reviews all describe the same failure mode: push notifications either never arrive, stop arriving after some time, or the entire sync process stalls silently and requires a manual restart. Several specific patterns:

  • Setup-correct, still broken. Multiple users explicitly note they followed documentation, checked every permission, disabled battery optimization — and it still doesn't work. Most damaging category because support replies often assume user error.
  • OEM background process kill. One Samsung S22 Ultra reviewer (v17.0, writing "Galaxy S26" — likely a typo) describes a specific failure: push connection stays active, battery usage spikes, then sync stops entirely. Only force-quit fixes it. This is One UI's aggressive background process management interacting badly with the push keep-alive.
  • Flagship hardware. Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra, S25 Ultra, OnePlus 11, Google Pixel 9. Not low-end devices. These users have tried to make it work.
  • Long-term desktop users as the canary. Two reviews cite 10–20 years of Thunderbird desktop loyalty as setup for disappointment. One: "I'm a tech person… if I can't easily make this app work, it's essentially unusable for most people." Highest-trust segment, highest churn risk.
  • K-9 proportionally harder hit. 7 of K-9's 77 March reviews (9.1%) vs. 24 of TB's 374 (6.4%).
  • 4★ reviews still reporting failures. Several gave 4★ despite broken push — fixing it would likely flip them to 5★.

These 5 reviews are a distinct bug from Problem A: notifications that won't clear after the email is read, emails reappearing on the notification slider after being dismissed, and mark-as-read not syncing to the notification shade. Users experiencing this may have push delivery working fine — the failure is in notification state management. Worth tracking separately from delivery failures, and already present in both v16.1 and v17.0.
