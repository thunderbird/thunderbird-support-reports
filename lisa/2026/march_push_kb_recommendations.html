Why this article exists

The #1 driver of push failure is not a bug in Thunderbird — it's OEM battery management killing the background process. The fix varies significantly by manufacturer. Current dev replies include generic steps that often don't resolve the issue. A single linkable article with OEM-specific instructions would let replies stay under 350 chars and be far more actionable.

Outline

  • Samsung (One UI): Apps → Battery → Unrestricted; Background usage limits → Never sleeping apps; App power management → Sleeping apps; Adaptive Battery
  • OnePlus (OxygenOS): Battery optimization → Don't optimize; Apps → Battery → Allow background; App freeze
  • Xiaomi / POCO (MIUI / HyperOS): Battery saver → No restrictions; App battery saver; Start in background; Security app
  • Motorola: Battery optimization → Don't optimize
  • Huawei: App launch → Manage manually → all three toggles
  • Sony (Xperia): STAMINA mode → disable or whitelist
  • Fairphone / stock Android: Apps → Battery → Unrestricted
  • Google Pixel: Apps → Battery → Unrestricted (Adaptive Battery)
  • Note: screenshots for Samsung and Xiaomi especially — UI changes across versions
  • Note: Android 15 introduced new background process restrictions — add a section when confirmed

Sample reply using this article

On Samsung, One UI's battery management can block sync even when permissions look correct. Our step-by-step guide for your device: [link] — please try all steps listed for Samsung. Let us know if it still doesn't work!

Why this article exists

Five reviews in March describe the same v17.0 regression: notifications that don't clear after the email is read, emails reappearing on the notification slider, and mark-as-read not syncing to the notification shade. These users have working push delivery — the failure is in notification state management. A known issue article lets dev replies link to it and sets expectation for a fix.

Outline

  • What the bug is: notification for an email persists even after the email is marked read or deleted from the notification shade
  • Affected versions: v17.0 (confirmed)
  • Status: under investigation / fix expected in v17.x (update when known)
  • Workaround: manually dismiss notifications from the shade; or temporarily revert to v16.x via F-Droid if critical
  • Link to GitHub issue for tracking

Sample reply using this article

This is a known issue in v17.0 that we're actively working on — notifications not clearing after reading. You can follow progress and find a workaround here: [link]. We appreciate your patience!

Several reviewers express confusion about the relationship between the two apps. This article reduces friction for long-term K-9 users encountering unexpected behaviour.

Outline

  • What stayed the same: account setup, IMAP/POP3 support, folder management
  • What changed: push notification setup (new UI), Thunderbird branding, new features coming
  • Why some K-9 users see push regressions (push architecture changed)
  • How to get help if things that worked in K-9 no longer work
