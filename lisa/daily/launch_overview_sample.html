Bottom line for Support Ops

Early Bird misdirects closed quickly and pulled the overall median down; real subscriber work — especially DNS and GH-linked bugs — takes longer. 39% of solves sit 3–7 business days in Pending, Research, or Snooze while eng ships fixes or customers propagate DNS. Subscribers see calendar days; plan comms and SLAs accordingly.

Open (New / Open)
requester_wait_time − on_hold
Ticket is in the agent queue — Unanswered or In Progress in our workflow. SLA running. Customer is waiting for us to reply or act. Median 4.0 business hours; first reply typically lands in ~2h.
Pending
agent_wait_time
We asked the customer for something — reply, DNS test, billing confirmation. SLA paused. Zendesk confusingly names this field “agent wait” (the agent is waiting, not working). Median 5.2 business hours.
Hold (On-hold)
on_hold_time
Waiting on engineering or a third party — Research status in our workflow, GitHub-linked bugs, infrastructure fixes. Median 0h (most tickets never hold), but 16 of 77 tickets used Hold; median 26h among those.

Mapping our statuses → Zendesk API metrics
Open (Unanswered / In Progress) → counted in requester_wait_time (customer waiting on us).
Pending → counted in agent_wait_time (we waiting on customer — yes, the API name is backwards).
Research (and some Snooze) → underlying Zendesk Hold → on_hold_time. The API cannot split Research vs Snooze vs Hold — all roll into one bucket.
Snooze without Hold underlying status may appear in Pending or Open depending on agent workflow — treat status hygiene as part of metric accuracy.

Glossary

AHT (Average Handle Time)
Time from ticket creation to resolution (Solved status). We report business hours first — Zendesk schedule 8am–8pm Eastern (12 hours/day), which excludes nights and weekends; calendar time adds nights/weekends and reads higher (~5 calendar days vs ~3 business days median). Median is used rather than mean to reduce outlier impact. Based on 77 solved tickets. See Where the time goes for the status breakdown.
Open · Pending · Hold (Zendesk metrics)
Open/New — ticket in the agent queue (Unanswered or In Progress). Tracked as requester_wait_time minus Hold. Customer is waiting for us. Median 4.0 business hours.
Pending — waiting on the customer to reply or test. Tracked as agent_wait_time (Zendesk's name reflects that the agent is waiting, not that agents are slow). Median 5.2 business hours.
Hold — waiting on engineering or a third party (Research in our workflow). Tracked as on_hold_time. Median 0h overall; 21% of tickets use Hold (median 26h when they do). The API cannot split Research vs Snooze — both map to underlying Hold when configured that way.
Ticket statuses (TB Pro workflow)
Open – Unanswered / Open – In Progress — SLA running (active work). Snooze 24h / Snooze 96h — paused, waiting on yourself or a colleague. Research — paused, needs investigation or dev input (auto-reopens in 48h). Pending — paused, waiting on the user (automation bumps then auto-solves). Solved — SLA stopped. Full SLA table, decision guide, and API mapping: Status guide tab in Resolution time.
First Reply Time
Time from ticket creation to the agent's first response. Reported in business hours (8am–8pm ET) with calendar time noted where relevant.
Contact Rate
Percentage of invitees who opened a support ticket. Calculated as: tickets ÷ invitees in that wave. A lower contact rate indicates a smoother onboarding experience.
Day-2 Peak
Historically, ~40% of a wave's first-week tickets arrive on the second day after invites go out. Used to estimate staffing needs for a new batch.
Misdirected — wrong product / non-subscriber
Tickets from people who do not have a Thundermail account and contacted support by mistake (e.g. desktop Thunderbird users, people who found the form via search). These are redirected to the correct channel and do not reflect subscriber support demand. 35 tickets (28% of classified themes) during Early Bird — a routing issue fixed May 19 (accounts#834). Flight 2: 1 misdirect in 32 tickets. Shown in a collapsed historical section on the themes panel, not in the Flight 2 baseline.
Cumulative contact rate
Running total of tickets divided by total invitees sent at that point in time. Drops sharply when a large new invite wave goes out (more invitees, same tickets).
